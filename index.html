I'm interested in any kind of research, especially machine intelligence and interpretability. I'm working on Mantis, a cognitive cartography platform by the Kellis Lab at MIT CSAIL.


Previously, I skipped two years of high school, spending one of which conducting research on solar cells. I also spent a summer at Wolfram and Euler Circle learning about cellular automata and differential geometry.


I grew up in Thailand and lived in a military camp. My time was spent chasing whatever I find interesting. I competed in olympiads, enjoyed science fairs, stumbled into research, then taught myself mental chess and blindfolded piano! I LOVE Minecraft and gummy bears. I'm currently learning how to shuffle cards in cool ways and wonder if a robot can do it...


I love fundamental questions and the endless forms they come in. I think it's comforting knowing that the mind is capable of so much more than itself.
